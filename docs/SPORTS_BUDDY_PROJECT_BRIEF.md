# SPORTS BUDDY APP - ONE PAGE PROJECT BRIEF

## 1. Product Vision
A real-time sports matching app where people can quickly find players or teams nearby for games like pickleball, badminton, tennis, and cricket.

## 2. Core Problem
Many people want to play but cannot find the right partner/team at the right time and location.

## 3. Target Users
1. Solo players looking for a partner
2. Teams looking for opponents
3. Casual and competitive players in local areas

## 4. MVP Scope (Phase 1)
1. User profile with sports, skill level, and play style
2. Availability status: available now, today, this weekend
3. Location-based discovery with distance filters
4. Match requests: send, accept, reject
5. Team challenge posts: team vs team
6. Basic in-app chat after match request acceptance
7. Network or friends list to reconnect with players
8. Privacy controls for who can view availability

## 5. Safety and Trust (Must Have)
1. Report and block users
2. Hide exact location until both users agree
3. Consent-based visibility settings
4. Optional gender preference filter controlled by each user
5. No dating or hotel meetup feature in Phase 1

## 6. Future Expansion (Phase 2 and Beyond)
1. Trekking and activity groups
2. Club and turf partnerships
3. Tournaments and paid events
4. Verified badges and premium discovery boosts

## 7. Recommended Tech Stack
1. Mobile app: Flutter
2. Backend: NestJS with TypeScript
3. Database: PostgreSQL with PostGIS
4. Realtime: WebSockets and Redis
5. Cloud: AWS (start simple, scale later)
6. Notifications: FCM and APNs
7. Maps and geolocation: Google Maps APIs

## 8. Monetization Plan
1. Premium visibility boosts
2. Verified player subscription
3. Partner promotions with local sports venues
4. Tournament hosting fees

## 9. 8-Week MVP Build Plan
1. Week 1-2: Product flows, UI wireframes, architecture
2. Week 3-4: Auth, profile, sports preferences, availability
3. Week 5: Nearby discovery and location filtering
4. Week 6: Requests, chat, team matching
5. Week 7: Network, block/report, moderation basics
6. Week 8: Pilot launch in one city with two sports

## 10. Success Metrics for Pilot
1. Daily active users
2. Match request acceptance rate
3. Matches completed per week
4. 7-day retention
5. Report rate and safety incident rate
